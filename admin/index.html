Tutorial & Best Practices

The recommended order for managing a client engagement from setup through follow-up.

Use this workflow for a clean record trail. Each step includes a shortcut to the correct admin area.

Recommended Workflow
12 Steps
Client → estimate → workshop → messages → billing → assessments → groups → follow-up

Complete Engagement Workflow

Open any step for instructions
1Confirm settings and rates
Before preparing financial documents, confirm your business information, payment instructions, invoice footer, and loaded rates.
  • Profile information appears on estimates and invoices.
  • Rates are maintained in Google Sheets and displayed here for verification.
2Create the client record
Create one client record for the organization before creating related work.
  • Use the organization’s consistent official name.
  • Save the primary contact, email, phone, and useful relationship notes.
  • Reuse the existing client instead of creating duplicates.
3Prepare and send the estimate
Prepare the estimate before scheduling the workshop. Review the expected participant count, hours, preparation, assessment fees, and discounts before sending.
  • Preview the estimate before delivery.
  • Update the saved estimate instead of creating a second copy for revisions.
  • Wait for acceptance before confirming the workshop date.
4Create and schedule the workshop
After the estimate is accepted, select the saved client and enter the confirmed workshop date, type, participant estimate, delivery format, contact details, and status.
  • Link the accepted estimate to the workshop.
  • For historical workshops, use a descriptive timeframe or leave the date unknown.
  • Keep the workshop status current from Scheduled through Completed.
5Create and manage the invoice
Create the invoice from the accepted estimate, then verify issue date, due date, recipient, totals, and payment instructions.
  • Record partial payments as they arrive.
  • Mark the invoice Paid only when the balance is satisfied.
  • Use Void or Canceled instead of deleting a legitimate financial record.
6Import or assemble assessment results
From the workshop, choose Assessment Results. Upload the official Individual Results workbook, or use Add Individual Results to assemble a roster from people already in the assessment library.
  • Review validation messages, then scroll through the complete participant preview before importing a workbook.
  • Use Merge for additions or corrections that should preserve omitted people.
  • Use Replace only when the uploaded workbook should become the complete active roster.
  • A manually assembled roster can still select a leader and generate a Team Map.
7Select the leader and review the Team View
Choose and save the team leader, then review all six Team View cards. The leader is intentionally represented twice in Genius and Frustration lists to reflect leadership influence; the person’s stored result is not duplicated.
8Preview and save the Team Map
Preview the Team Map, choose the preferred name format, and check all six Genius and Frustration sections.
  • Use Print / Save as PDF to store the map locally.
  • Team Map PDFs are not stored in Google Sheets or Google Drive by the app.
9Add individuals and maintain reusable groups
Use Assessments & Groups to add an individual result received outside a workshop, or combine individuals and complete workshop rosters without duplicating anyone’s assessment.
  • For an ad hoc result, enter the person’s name and assign all six cards to Genius, Competency, or Frustration.
  • At the top of Group Builder, choose a saved group and use Load Group to edit its members, leader, name, or description.
  • Choose a group-specific leader, then use Save & Create Team Map for a direct preview.
  • Delete Group is available in the saved-group manager and on every saved-group card.
  • If a group is deleted by mistake, use Restore Group under Recently Deleted Groups.
  • Review possible duplicate names when assessment data conflicts.
10Review Assessment Analytics
Use the analytics page to review unique people served, individual Genius distribution, and all 15 Genius pairings. Filter by client, workshop, dates, Genius, or participant search; every count and percentage updates with the filtered list.
  • A person appearing in multiple workshops counts once within the filtered view.
  • Each person has two Geniuses, so the six individual percentages normally total approximately 200%.
  • Each person has one pairing, and reversed order such as WI and IW is counted together.
11Complete follow-up and maintain records
Update the workshop status, follow-up date, invoice status, and notes after the engagement.
  • Archive inactive business records to reduce clutter while preserving history.
  • Use permanent deletion only for genuine mistakes or test records.
  • Assessment removal leaves the workshop and financial documents intact and retains inactive import history.
12Use reusable email templates
Use Email Templates whenever you need a recurring workshop message. Select a template, personalize its placeholders, review unresolved values, and copy the plain-text result into your email provider.
  • From a workshop card, Use Email Template prefills available organization, contact, schedule, location, participant count, and leader details.
  • Templates never send email; you remain in control of the final recipient and message.
  • Archive old templates to preserve them without cluttering the active library.

Best Practices at a Glance

Keep one source of truth

Link workshops, estimates, and invoices. Update existing records instead of making duplicates.

Preview before sending

Confirm names, dates, totals, recipients, and payment details before delivering any document.

Protect history

Prefer archive, replace, remove, void, or cancel workflows when the record represents real activity.

Refresh after external changes

If data is edited directly in Google Sheets, refresh the relevant admin page before continuing.
